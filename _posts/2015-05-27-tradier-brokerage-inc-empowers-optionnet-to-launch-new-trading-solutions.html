Tradier Brokerage, Inc., the leading cloud-based financial services brokerage firm, today announced that options trading provider OptionNET has integrated with the Tradier API. OptionNET Explorer is a complete options trading and analysis software platform. Customers can now use OptionNET Explorer for free to access options analysis technology, and also execute trades at $0.35 per option contract and $3.49 per equity trade. The integration will empower the trading tool platform to become a one stop shop for traders, with direct access to brokerage technology and unique trading solutions.

Tradier is an accelerator of innovation for fintech organizations looking to launch new solutions. The Tradier Brokerage API is democratizing access with cost effective brokerage execution and technology, making it possible to open up access to new entrants without having to cross the barrier of legacy companies.

The Tradier integration enables OptionNET to offer exceptional pricing and free subscription options to users. The new relationship will also enable OptionNET to enhance services with a data source that provides a no cost option.

“We believe in the democratization of the financial services industry and empowering new solutions to go to market, which will ultimately bring much needed innovation to the community,” said Steve Agalloco, VP of Engineering of Tradier, Inc. “OptionNET shares our vision of elevating the investor and trading experience with unique service options. By adopting our open brokerage API and Tradier cloud technology, OptionNET can offer a seamless trading experience, quickly and comprehensively.”

“We’re excited about the new options we can provide our user base with the Tradier Brokerage integration. By connecting with Tradier Brokerage, we are able to offer value and choice to our trader community,” said Andy Mitchell, Managing Partner at OptionNET LLP. “The Tradier API was simple to integrate and allows us to expand our services, creating a seamless experience and new functionality to our user community.”
