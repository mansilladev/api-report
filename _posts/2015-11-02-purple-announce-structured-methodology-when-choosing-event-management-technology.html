When searching for technology based event management solution's there are a number of common factors issues to be taken into consideration. Given the complexity, infrastructure and payment methods modern users demand, it makes good business sense to work with an experienced technology consultancy. Finding the right solution can be a daunting task considering the vast number of options available in today’s market. At Purple we have developed a proven methodology to map and prioritise any company’s event management requirements and transpose this into the identification of the right solution.    

How can Purple help companies select the right Event Management Technology from the vast list of current providers?

At Purple our proven methodology to find the right solution first involves undertaking a fit/gap requirements analysis. This is constructed by interviewing key stakeholders within businesses. We then prioritise users requirements and compare these against a carefully prepared matrix of all the major market solutions. This allows Purple to measure and score each solution against the requirements and make an objective recommendation.

Below is a list of just some of the factors which form the comparison matrix:

Functionality

  • Can the solution be easily integrated into the host’s website via iFrame/widgets?
  • Is the attendee registration process customisable?
  • Is the solution a standalone piece of software which must be deployed on each user’s machine? Or is it an online cloud based system making it accessible anywhere in the world?
  • Is there the ability to setup organisation hierarchy with appropriate security/ access rights?
  • Is the solution capable of issuing personalised and targeted communications?
  • Does the solution offer an extensive API to enable the sharing of event/user data between the Data Warehouse, CRM and Finance systems ?
  • Can HTML emails be generated easily without coding?
  • Is the in built reporting/analytics comprehensive and easy to use?
  • Is the solution capable of emailing invitations, confirmations and reminders?
  • Does the solution have the inbuilt functionality to launch email marketing campaigns and deliverability reporting?
  • Is the solution aesthetically pleasing to the end user?
  • Does the solution provide the user with a familiar environment?

The Provider

  • Does the provider have sufficient development and support staff?
  • Is the provider stable in the industry and likely to have any large change to the service in the foreseeable future?
  • How long has the provider been in business and are they profitable?
  • Do you have protection against future price increases?

The Cost

  • Does the solution have automated payment refund capabilities?
  • Does the provider charge a percentage on ticket sales and credit card transactions?
  • Can you use the registration system to collect payments in an automated and secure manner?
  • Are fees disclosed in a customised proposal or contract?
  • Does the solution offer mobile capabilities?
